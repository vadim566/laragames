<h2>/*E. Substitution.*/||</h2>
/*<i>You will now·hear a model sentence followed by a cue
Use this cue to make your new sentence. Like this:</i>*/||

/*<i>You will hear:</i>*/|| nyangatja palya|/|palatja||
/*<i>You will say:</i>*/|| palatja palya.||

/*<i>Then you listen for the correct response and repeat
it. This is called a four phase drill, because of
the four items:</i>*/||

/*<i>1. The model:</i>*/|| nyangatja palya|/|palatja||
/*<i>2. Your attempt:</i>*/|| palatja palya||
/*<i>3. The confirmation:</i>*/|| palatja palya||
/*<i>4. Your repetition:</i>*/|| palatja palya||

/*<i>Join in as soon as you understand.</i>*/||

/*<b>First model.</b>*/||

<h2>/*F. Translation into the language.*/||</h2>
/*<i>You will hear an English statement. Put it into the native language
and listen out for the correct response. Then repeat the correct response.
Off you go.</i>*/||

/*<i>The man is sick.</i>*/|| wati pikatjara.||
/*<i>The child is sick.</i>*/|| tjitji pikatjara.||
/*<i>The child is well.</i>*/|| tjitji palya.||

/*<i>That one distant is sick.</i>*/|| nyaratja pikatjara.||
/*<i>That one distant is well.</i>*/|| nyaratja palya.||
